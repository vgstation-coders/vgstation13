Fission Reactors: a quick guide

Though often said to be old, outdated technology, fission power should not be underestimated. Even a modest reactor is capible of powering a medium-sized station for several hours with no upkeep. The dismissal of fission technology has delayed the uncovering of more recent discoveries in material sciences which may have been known decades earlier.

What you'll need

building a reactor is an expensive endeavor, costing a lot of both time and resources. For the most basic of reactor designs, here is the bare minimum needed to set one up and start generating power. Most of the materials are either provided in the starter kit, or should be available on-site.
  • 69 sheets of plasteel
  • 42 metal rods
  • 15 lengths of wire
  • 3 mater bins
  • 2 scanning modules
  • 2 micro-manipulators
  • 1 console screen
  • fissile material and a fuel rod (provided in the starter kit)
  • circuit boards for the controller, fuel rod, and control rod assemblies
  • piping (partially included)
  • at least 1 thermoelectric generator (not included)
  • a welder, crowbar, screwdriver, wrench, and wirecutters (not included)
additionally, to make a isotopic separational combiner (the machine used to fill and additionally recycle spent fuel) you will need the following materials (provided in the starter kit):
  • 5 sheets of metal
  • 5 lengths of wire
  • 2 mater bins
  • 2 scanning modules
  • 1 micro-manipulator
  • 1 console screen
  • the associated circuit board
  • piping (not included)

How to construct the parts

Reactor casing

  1. use 4 plasteel to construct a reactor casing frame in the desired location
  2. insert 4 metal rods inside of the frame
  3. use a screwdriver to fasten the rods
  4. (optional) insert a straight pipe into the frame to make it a coolant port
  5. (optional) use a crowbar to change the direction of the port
  6. apply 2 plasteel as external plating
  7. weld the external plating to the frame

Control & Fuel rod assembly

  1. ise 5 plasteel to construct a reinforced machine frame in the desired location
  2. add 5 lengths of wiring to the machine
  3. insert the corresponding circuit board
  4. insert 2 metal rods inside of the frame
  5. add a matter bin to the frame
  6. if control rod: add a micro-manipulator
    if fuel rod: add a scanning module
  7. use a screwdriver to finish assembly

Reactor controller

  1. use 5 plasteel to construct a reinforced machine frame in the desired location
  2. add 5 lengths of wiring to the machine
  3. insert the circuit board
  4. insert 2 metal rods inside of the frame
  5. add a matter bin, micro-manipulator, scanning module, and a console screen to the frame
  6. use a screwdriver to finish assembly

Isotopic separational combiner

  1. Use 5 metal to construct a machine frame in the desired location
  2. add 5 lengths of wiring to the machine
  3. insert the circuit board
  4. add a 2 matter bins, micro-manipulator, 2 scanning modules, and a console screen to the frame
  5. use a screwdriver to finish assembly

Design considerations

The design of a nuclear reactor is very important. build it wrong, and you may find yourself having a meltdown.

casing

the casing of a reactor should include the whole perimeter of it, with no gaps.

coolant ports

a reactor should have at least 2 ports, one for a coolant input, and another for output. coolant ports can be placed at any point in the casing.

controller

a reactor can only have 1 controller to it, and must be placed at a corner of a reactor.

fuel rods

fuel rods should be placed in the interior of the reactor. for each cardinally-adjacent fuel rod, a fuel rod will gain bonus power production without affecting fuel duration. Fuel duration is only affected by the number of fuel rod assemblies.

control rods

control rods are able to affect fuel rods in every ordinal direction around them. if a control rod is not affecting a fuel rod, then the reaction is unable to be stopped. it is recommended to have all fuel rods be controlled for safety reasons.

How to disassemble

IMPORTANT: before disassembling a reactor, drain all coolant first, and make sure that it is not currently undergoing fission. Failure to do so may result in injury, destruction of property, or even death.

Reactor casing

  1. use a welder to detach the external plating from the frame
  2. use a crowbar to pry off the plating from the frame
  3. (optional) use a wrench to remove the piping if there is any
  4. use a screwdriver to loosen the internal rods
  5. use wirecutters to remove the rods
  6. use a wrench to disassemble the frame

Control & Fuel rod assembly, reactor controller

  1. use a welder to detach the external plating from the frame
  2. use a crowbar to remove the internal components from the frame
  3. use wirecutters to remove the wiring from the frame
  4. use a wrench to disassemble the frame.

Isotopic separational combiner

  1. use a screwdriver to open the maintenance hatch
  2. use a crowbar to pry out the electronics
  3. use wirecutters to remove the wiring from the frame
  4. use a wrench to disassemble the frame.

Other notes

If you unscrew the maintenance hatch of a fuel rod assembly, you can add 4 metal sheets to it. Doing so will make the assembly not give any bonuses for adjacent fuel rods. You can undo this by removing the sheets with a crowbar.
